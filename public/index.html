Jobs in the
Zooniverse

Working for the Zooniverse means working with an interesting group of people on some of the most interesting projects around, while knowing your work will be seen by hundreds of thousands of people and will contribute to the progress of knowledge. It’s a good way to spend your time.

The Zooniverse is a collaboration between institutions from the UK and USA; all of our team are employed by one or the other of these partner institutions. This page will be updated to provide up to date summaries of Zooniverse-related jobs. For more information, follow the instructions in each job description or get in touch with the named contact.

We hope to hear from you soon.

Oxford

The Zooniverse team at the University of Oxford are leading the development of the new Zooniverse platform, as well as projects covering everything from Penguins to Particle Physics, all the way up to disaster relief. We encourage flexible working, including working remotely for part of each week.

Developer

The Oxford Internet Institute and the Zooniverse are looking for a full-time Developer to work on a newly funded project, which will harness the Zooniverse volunteer crowd to identify species of African wildlife from camera trap photos. This is an exciting opportunity to be part of a talented and friendly team of developers and academics making real contributions to scientific research.

Chicago

The Adler Planetarium's Citizen Science team includes a dynamic mix of web developers, data scientists, researchers and educators who are responsible for everything from building Zooniverse projects and developing governance tools for volunteers, to running Hack Days and teen programs. We can offer flexible hours and the opportunity to try new things, whether that be getting involved with outreach or playing with cutting edge technology.

Web Developer

The Citizen Science department at the Adler Planetarium is seeking an experienced full-stack developer to build web apps and infrastructure for the Zooniverse (www.zooniverse.org), home to the web's best and most popular citizen science projects.

As a Zooniverse developer, you’ll work with our team of talented and friendly developers and support staff to build unique, intuitive, and user-friendly web apps that enable anyone to participate in real research online. You’ll work on a variety of projects to support our cutting-edge platform that helps make real contributions to research in the sciences and humanities.

Visual Designer

The Citizen Science department at the Adler Planetarium is seeking an experienced designer to lead visual design, UX, and UI decision making and implementation for the Zooniverse (www.zooniverse.org), home to the web's best and most popular citizen science projects.

As the Visual Designer, you’ll be responsible for designing intuitive, beautiful, and user-friendly web apps that enable anyone to participate in real research online. You will also have a huge impact on the future of design in the Zooniverse and be responsible for developing and maintaining the Zooniverse brand.

Minnesota

The group in the School of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Minnesota is leading the development of the Zooniverse's text-based projects.

Other

Our work involves collaboration with a vast array of partners from across the world. When they have jobs that involve working with the Zooniverse, we'll post them here.

Citizen Science Research Associate and Ogden Science Officer

The post comprises of the roles of Citizen Science Research Associate (0.5FTE) and Outreach Officer (0.5FTE), the latter incorporates the roles of Ogden Science Officer and South East Physics Network (SEPnet) Outreach Officer.

The OU are leading the Dissemination, Education, and Citizen Science work package of the EU Horizon2020 project 'Astronomy ESFRI and Research Infrastructure Clusters' (ASTERICS). A major activity of this work package is to create mass participation experiments and educational resources targeting the science-inclined public of college age and over, working with the Zooniverse team to build them. You will arrange international workshops to facilitate the design and delivery of the citizen science experiments, and act as the project manager for the experiments.
